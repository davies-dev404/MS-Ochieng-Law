# Cybersecurity Best Practices: M.S. Ochieng Law Firm

As a provider of elite legal services, the security of client data is part of our strategic mandate. This guide outlines the mandatory protocols for all firm personnel to ensure the integrity and confidentiality of our digital infrastructure.

## 1. Data Classification & Handling
### [HIGH PRIORITY]
*   **Privileged Communications**: All emails containing legal advice or client data must be handled through the firm's encrypted email system (`ochiengm@msochienglaw.co.ke`).
*   **File Storage**: Never store client files on personal cloud accounts (Dropbox, Google Personal). Use the firm's securePractice Management System.
*   **Hard Copy Disposal**: All physical documents containing client data must be shredded; never dispose of client records in general waste.

## 2. Password & Identity Management
*   **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**: MFA must be enabled on all institutional accounts (Email, Banking, Case Management).
*   **Encryption**: Ensure your company-issued devices (Laptops, Tablets) have full-disk encryption enabled (BitLocker or FileVault).
*   **Password Complexity**: Use a minimum of 14 characters, including symbols and numbers. Use a secure password manager for all firm credentials.

## 3. Communication Security
*   **Public Wi-Fi**: Never access the firm's backend or client files via public, unsecured Wi-Fi. Use a VPN or a secure mobile hotspot.
*   **Email Phishing**: Be vigilant. M.S. Ochieng Law Firm will never ask for your password via email. Verify any unusual requests for fund transfers via a secondary, trusted channel (phone call).

## 4. Incident Response
*   **Reporting**: If you suspect a data breach, lost device, or phishing attempt, report it immediately to the Strategic Technology lead.
*   **Client Notification**: We maintain a 24-hour notification mandate for any verified breach affecting client data.
